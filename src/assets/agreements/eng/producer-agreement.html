List of Terms

The Producer agrees it has read this list of terms.

1. . U.S. Foreign Corrupt Practices Act (FCPA) - A law which prohibits the company, its employees and agents from offering, paying, or promising to pay a bribe to a foreign official in order to obtain or retain business or an unfair advantage over a competitor. The FCPA applies to any U.S. company and its employees as well as its non-U.S. subsidiaries or employees and to those doing business on their behalf.

2. U.K. Bribery Act - A U.K. law which generally makes it a criminal offense to bribe another person or company; (ii) to request or receive a bribe; (iii) or when a corporate entity fails to prevent bribery by a person acting on its behalf; or (iv) a person or company bribes a foreign public official. The UK Bribery Act applies to any U.K. person or company, or any company or person conducting business in, on behalf of, or from the U.K., regardless of where the crime occurred.

3. Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC) - Office within the U.S. Department Treasury that maintains a list that prohibits US companies, their affiliated companies and U.S. persons from transacting business in sanctioned countries, or with any person or entity maintained on its list (potential terrorists, narco-traffickers, etc.) Because Bupa Insurance Company (BIC) is a U.S. company and because Bupa Insurance Limited-Florida Branch (BINS)ù Latin American portfolio is serviced from U.S. Companies and by U.S. employees, they must comply with this law.

4. Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA) - A U.S. law which sets forth specific privacy and security regulations relating to a customerùs Protected Health Information (ùPHIù). PHI is individually identifiable information such as policyholder and dependent name(s) address(es) and account numbers, as well as past, present and future health/medical information and payment information for health services. As Florida licensed insurance companies, BIC and BINS are required by this law to keep PHI confidential and can be fined by the government if it inadvertently (or intentionally) discloses PHI to a third party. HIPAA also requires that insurance companies, like BIC and BINS, sign Business Associate and Privacy Services Agreements with all third parties (not just agents, but also with vendors) that have access to PHI.

5. Business Associate and Privacy Services Agreement- Is an agreement signed with another company or individual that is performing an activity on behalf of a U.S. insurance company, during which it will have access to customerùs PHI.
